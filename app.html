Radio-Frequency Interference (RFI) filter dataflow implementation

The process involves filtering Radio Frequency Interference (RFI) from an acquisition file obtained by a radio telescope. The file is in the ".dada" format (DADA stands for Distributed Acquisition and Data Analysis) and is comprised of two parts: the header, which contains information about the radio telescope, and the data part. The data part consists of complex numbers. The first step of the process is to separate the real and imaginary components of the data to apply filters to both. Two filters are computed simultaneously, and one is applied to the data.

  • The first filter is the Standard Deviation (STD) filter. See Wiki STD deviation.

    \[ \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{1}{N} \sum\limits_{i=1}^N (x_i - \mu)} \]

  • The second filter is the Median Average Deviation (MAD) filter. See Wiki MAD deviation.

    \[ {\displaystyle MAD = \mathrm{median} (|X_{i}-{\tilde {X}}|)} \]

Both filters aim to find a threshold and remove data points above this threshold. Finally, the filtered real and imaginary parts are combined using their conjugates to reconstruct the complex numbers. These reconstructed complex numbers are then used to generate a new ".dada" file.
