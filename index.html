Welcome to the home page of my website.

I am a post-doctoral researcher at ENS Rennes, with a joint appointment at SATIE, ENS Paris Saclay. I obtained my PhD in Signal, Image, Vision at INSA Rennes, with a joint appointment at the IETR laboratory in 2024. My current research efforts concentrate on rapid prototyping for HPC systems deploying astronomical applications using dataflow optimization. My work is supported by the ANR project Dark Era, which aims to build SimSDP, an exascale simulation tool designed for early analysis of the SKA radio telescope. The SKA project, a major advancement in radio astronomy, consists of antennas and dishes located in South Africa and Australia. It converts voltages into visibility through the CSP pipeline processor and transforms these visibilities into output images using SDP processors, processing several terabytes per second. This poses a significant resource allocation challenge, which I am addressing.

My research benefits from collaborations and knowledge exchanges through the Rising Stars initiative.
